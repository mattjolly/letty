Performance

As a multi-genre orchestral and chamber musician, Letty has performed in all of the major UK concert venues. She has worked with orchestras including BBC Concert Orchestra, Britten Sinfonia, National Symphony Orchestra, City of London Sinfonia, Royal Philharmonic Orchestra and English National Ballet, as well with English National Opera and English Touring Opera. Letty’s playing has been broadcast on BBC Radio 3 numerous times, including most recently with the Goldfield Ensemble on Hear and Now as well as In Tune. She has also worked on various projects with Apartment House contemporary music ensemble, performing on Radio France and recording new works, with upcoming performances at the Wigmore Hall in 2021 for a celebration of the work of Morton Feldman.

Letty works frequently with the Multi-Story Orchestra and has played for various projects with the London Contemporary Orchestra as well as Urban Soul Orchestra.

Commercial

She has collaborated with commercial artists including Badly Drawn Boy, Charlotte Church, Kate Tempest, Laura Mvula, Sam Lee, Squarepusher and Shirley Bassey, and has toured with artists including Tony Hadley/Spandau Ballet, ABC and The Slow Show, as well as several arena tours for projects including The Sessions (UK/Europe), Il Divo (UK) and Ajay Atul (India).

Festivals

Letty has performed at festivals ranging from the Oxford Contemporary Music and Cheltenham Literary Festivals in the UK, to the Sud-Tirol Jazz and Haizetara Festivals in Europe; as well as at high-profile festivals including the Edinburgh Festival and Glastonbury.

Training

Letty was a member of Southbank Sinfonia (2013 season) and was fortunate to work with conductors including Edward Gardner, Charles Hazlewood, Barry Wordsworth and Paul Wynne Griffiths, as well as performing with soloists such as Guy Barker, Ryan Wigglesworth and Sir Thomas Allen. Letty performed in a production of El Gato En Botas as part of the Jette Parker Young Artists Scheme in the Royal Opera House Linbury Theatre, as well as performing Schubert’s Octet in the Royal Opera House Crush Room.

Letty was part of the Southbank Centre’s The Rest is Noise festival (2013), with an historic performance of Frank Zappa’s colossal 200 Motels. She also performed in the Southbank Centre’s Meltdown Festival curated by Yoko Ono, and was delighted to be part of a performance of Sky Piece for Jesus Christ, in which the musicians are wrapped in bandages until they can play no more and the piece comes to its end...
