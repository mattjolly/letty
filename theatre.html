Theatre

Letty then went on to perform in the National Theatre’s production of Macbeth, starring Rory Kinnear and Anne-Marie Duff (including NT live broadcast to cinemas worldwide). Working closely with composer Orlando Gough, musical director Marc Tritschler and instrument-maker Simon Allen, the production involved a fascinating Research and Development opportunity to make new instruments that would feature in the play’s post-apocalyptic setting. As one of only two musicians in the production, Letty was involved with the creation of these new instruments from the start as well as being heavily involved with the composition process, which included learning new skills such as performing on bowed car-springs; using old organ pipes to create the sound of a castle asleep (for the soundtrack beneath Macbeth’s famous ‘sleep no more’ speech); and performing raucous calls on a ‘tri-horn’ with multiple bells and valve systems..!

Letty has worked with the Royal Shakespeare Company, performing in Julius Caesar as part of the RSC’s Rome Season. The score was composed by Mira Calix for live brass instruments with electronics and involved close-microphone techniques from the players, including breath noises and valve sounds.

Finally, Letty has performed for theatre shows as a member of the orchestra for productions including Little Miss Sunshine (Arcola Theatre, London), and The Lion King.
